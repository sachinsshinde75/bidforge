5.3.2 Cable-Stayed Span Construction Methodology

The 180m cable-stayed span will be executed using the cantilever method, building outward from both pylons toward the centre using temporary stay-cable arrangements. Erection will progress in 6m segments, with each segment cast in-situ over a movable steel form-traveller assembly. This approach draws directly from HCC's execution methodology on the Bogibeel Rail-Cum-Road Bridge (Assam, 2018) and the cable-stayed approach span at Bandra-Worli Sea Link.

Stay cables will be supplied by Freyssinet International and installed in stages, with iterative stress-monitoring at each cycle. Pylon construction will use jump-form shuttering with M60-grade self-compacting concrete. Geometry will be controlled through real-time GPS-aided survey at each segment, with form correction calculations performed by an external bridge-engineering consultant (anticipated: COWI A/S, Denmark, per HCC framework agreement).
