💬 RFP Q&A Chat
All answers cite RFP page + clause references
What does this RFP say about defect liability period?
DLP varies by work type. Standard works carry a 24-month DLP from issue of Taking-Over Certificate. The cable-stayed structure carries an extended 60-month DLP per SCC 28.4. Bank guarantee validity must cover DLP + 90 days per GCC 22.3.
📎 GCC 27.1 (p.94) · SCC 28.4 (p.71) · GCC 22.3 (p.91)
Is there a price variation clause? What's the formula?
Yes — SCC 14.7. PVC applies to works executed beyond 12 months from LOA. The formula uses general Wholesale Price Index (WPI) as the escalation basis. Note: steel and cement sub-indices have historically moved 2-4× faster than general WPI; consider Q-003 to negotiate sub-indices.
📎 SCC 14.7 (p.65) · Schedule G escalation tables (p.78)
What's the cable-stayed span specification?
180m main span, A-frame pylons. The cable-stayed structure is specified in Vol IV §4.2. Main span 180m with two side spans of 60m each (total 300m). Pylon height 78m above pier cap. Cable system: Freyssinet or equivalent (PSS strands, 15.7mm dia). Design speed 80 kmph. The Employer permits alternative design proposals per ITT 8.3.
📎 Vol IV §4.2 (p.142) · ITT 8.3 (p.18)
Performance BG requirement?
5% of contract value, furnished within 28 days of LoA. Validity must extend to DLP + 90 days. If cumulative LD exceeds 2% of contract value, PBG escalates to 7.5%. Acceptable from any scheduled commercial bank.
📎 GCC 22.3 (p.91)

5.3 Cable-Stayed Bridge Erection Methodology

The cable-stayed bridge over the GMLR alignment shall be constructed using the balanced cantilever method with sequential cable stressing. The Joint Venture proposes to use Freyssinet PSS-15.7 stay cables, identical to those successfully deployed by HCC on the Bandra-Worli Sea Link, which has demonstrated long-term performance in Mumbai's marine environment.From: Bandra-Worli Sea Link bid §4.2.1

The pylon shall be cast in-situ using climbing formwork, with construction proceeding at 4.5 metres per cycle. The pylon legs converge to form an A-frame profile at +78m above the pier cap.RFP-derived: Vol IV §4.2 specs

Erection sequence: (i) pylon construction M14-M19, (ii) tower crane erection M19, (iii) starter segment over pier M20, (iv) balanced cantilever progression M20-M28 with stay cables installed sequentially, (v) closure pour at midspan M28, (vi) final stay tensioning M28-M29.Modified from: Bogibeel bid §3.6

The JV will engage IIT-Bombay's Department of Civil Engineering as Independent Design Review consultant per the requirements of SCC 12.3, with proof-checking completed before commencement of pylon foundation works. This represents an enhancement over the standard third-party review process, reflecting the JV's commitment to quality assurance for this critical structure.AI-original: written for this bid
