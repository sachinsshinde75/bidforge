5.3 Cable-Stayed Span Construction Methodology

5.3.1 Overview. The 180m cable-stayed main span over the Western Express Highway shall be executed using the cantilever construction method, building outward from both pylons toward the centre through a sequence of pre-engineered segments. This approach leverages HCC's proven execution methodology developed on the Bogibeel Rail-Cum-Road Bridge (2014-2018, Assam) and refined during the cable-stayed approach span of the Bandra-Worli Sea Link (2005-2009, Mumbai). Source: Bogibeel methodology · 87% match

5.3.2 Pylon Construction. The two A-frame pylons (height 78m above pier cap) shall be cast in-situ using climbing-form shuttering with M60 self-compacting concrete. Pylon construction will proceed in 4m lifts, with a target rate of one lift per 5 days. Reinforcement will be pre-fabricated in cages at the Bhandup pre-cast yard and lifted into position using two 200t crawler cranes deployed dedicated to the cable-stayed work front. Modified: yard location updated · crane spec increased

5.3.3 Segment Erection. Once pylons reach the deck level, cantilever erection of deck segments begins symmetrically from each pylon. Each segment is 6m long, pre-cast at the Bhandup yard, and weighs 95-110t depending on position. Segments are transported overnight to minimise traffic disruption, then lifted into position by two form-travellers operating in tandem. After geometric verification, segment is stressed against the previous and the corresponding pair of stay cables is installed.3 Source: Bogibeel + Sea Link · 73% match

5.3.4 Stay Cables. Stay cables shall be supplied by Freyssinet International (HCC framework partner since 2008), comprising 7-wire low-relaxation prestressing strands (15.7mm dia) encased in HDPE sheaths. Each stay assembly will be installed in 3 stages with iterative re-stressing as deck segments are added. Real-time stress monitoring will be conducted using vibrating-wire load cells, with data logged continuously to the project management system. Source: Bogibeel methodology · 91% match

5.3.5 Geometry Control. Given the precision required for cable-stayed construction, geometry will be controlled using a GPS-augmented total station survey at each erection cycle. Form correction calculations will be performed by an independent bridge-engineering consultant (anticipated COWI A/S, Denmark, per HCC's framework agreement signed in February 2024 for cable-stayed and cantilever-erected projects). All field measurements will be cross-validated against design predictions before stay-cable adjustment. New: COWI engagement specifically commissioned for GMLR

5.3.6 Quality Assurance. All concrete will conform to IS 456 with the additional requirements specified in Vol II §6.3.2. Steel reinforcement testing will follow IS 1786 and the contractor's QA/QC plan detailed in Vol V. Concrete cube testing will be conducted at 1-day, 7-day, and 28-day intervals, with results uploaded to BMC's e-Construction portal within 48 hours of test completion. Source: HCC standard QA/QC template · 96% match
